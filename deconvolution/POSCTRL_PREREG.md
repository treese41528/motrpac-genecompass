# Positive-control pre-registration — exhaustive per-cell-type DE

**Status: FROZEN before inspecting any per-gene DE result for these genes.** Committed as part of
the exhaustive `run_pseudobulk_de.R` work. The machine-readable spec is
`deconvolution/reference/posctrl_prereg.tsv` (105 committed gene×target rows), generated by
`deconvolution/build_posctrl_prereg.py`. The comparison (`compare_posctrl.py`) executes *only* this
frozen spec; it does **not** expand the gene list after seeing results.

Rationale (why pre-register): the named genes/programs are a clean positive control *because* we
commit to them — and to their expected directions — in advance. Scanning a paper's full supplement
for any overlap would inflate apparent concordance via the garden-of-forking-paths; that is a fishing
expedition, not a control. And because non-recovery is ambiguous between a pipeline problem and real
biology, the reading of a miss must be fixed *before* results, or the call is no longer clean.

## 1. The committed sets (see `posctrl_prereg.tsv`)

Three anchoring tiers, by how cleanly the **expression** direction is anchored:

| Tier | Source | What is anchored | How scored |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | MoTrPAC PASS1B main (Nature 2024 #21 / Cell Genomics #22) | expression direction WITH training (8w, sex-consistent unless noted) | **direction**: present + dose-significant + sign(8w logFC) matches `expected_dir` |
| **Ai** | MoTrPAC named, transcript direction *not* stated | identity + tissue (+sex) | present + training-DE in target; direction reported, not scored |
| **B** | Vetr 2024 named genes | identity + tissue + sex (paper "direction" is trait-level) | present + training-DE in matching tissue (+sex); direction reported |
| **C** | Yu 2023 immune programs | cell-type/program **responsiveness** only | program enrichment among the cell type's training-DE genes; **direction NOT scored** |

Tier-A direction anchors (the strongest controls): mitochondrial/OXPHOS + biogenesis + heat-shock
**UP at 8w, sex-consistent** in skeletal-muscle parenchyma (SKMVL/SKMGN *Skeletal muscle cells/fibers*)
and heart (*Cardiomyocytes*) — `Sod2, Slc2a4, Mef2c/a/d, Opa1, Mfn1, Prkab1, Tbc1d1, Plin2/4/5,
Hspa1b, Hsp90aa1`; liver metabolic **UP** in hepatocytes (`Stat3, Pxn, Hsp90aa1`); blood
haematopoietic-mobilization TFs **UP** (`Gabpa, Ets1, Klf3`); WAT adrenergic receptors **DOWN**
(`Adra1b/1d/2b, Adrb1`).

## 2. Definitions (frozen)

- **Significance (training/dose):** `FDR_IHW < 0.05` (the primary global IHW~tissue FDR on the Fisher
  sex-combined dose p). Per-block BH (`FDR_BH_block`) is reported as a secondary/local view.
- **Sex-specific significance:** per-sex BH on the 8w contrast (`fdr_8w_M` / `fdr_8w_F`) `< 0.05`.
- **Direction (Tier A):** sign of the combined sex-adjusted 8w contrast `lfc_8w` (the adapted state
  Vetr/MoTrPAC key on), corroborated by sign of `slope_week`. A Tier-A gene **recovers** iff it is
  testable + dose-significant + the 8w sign matches `expected_dir`.
- **Target match:** `cell_type_target = ANY` → any cell type in the tissue qualifies (report which);
  else the gene must hit one of the listed cell types. `sex = male|female` → require the matching
  per-sex test; `any` → use the combined test.
- **Tier-C responsiveness:** fraction of the program's testable genes that are training-DE
  (`FDR_IHW<0.05`) in the target cell type, vs the block-wide DE rate (one-sided enrichment). Reported
  with direction tallies but **scored only on enrichment**, never on a fixed direction.

## 3. The miss-ladder (frozen; resolve every non-recovery in this order)

1. **Coverage** — is the gene's ENSRNOG present/testable in that block (`n_nonzero ≥ 1`, i.e. it
   appears in the block's `de__*.tsv`)? If absent → reference/coverage limitation, **not** evidence
   about biology. Logged "not testable." *(Pre-declared not-testable now: `Foxp3` (absent from every
   pred_z), and `Gnly, Gzmb, Znf143, Ndufa13, S100a12` (absent from the gene universe) — `testable_prior=FALSE`.)*
2. **Power** — is the cell type abundant enough: `mean_fraction ≥ 0.01` **and** the gene's
   `n_nonzero ≥ 25` (≥50% of the 50 samples)? If not → rare-type/sink → "inconclusive," not "absent."
3. **Confound** — is the block's composition flag on (`frac_week_p < 0.05`)? If so, a hit *or* miss is
   read RELATIVE/differential (the cell fraction itself trends with dose).
4. **Biology** — only a gene that is covered + powered + unconfounded and still flat (or, for a
   direction-anchored Tier-A gene, moves opposite) is read as biology: the bulk effect is **not carried
   by this cell type** at cell-type resolution. No new post-hoc excuse for a specific miss is permitted
   outside this ladder.

## 4. Pre-stated expectations (so the result is interpretable either way)

- **Expected to recover (pipeline sanity):** Tier-A muscle/heart mito+heat-shock UP in the muscle/cardiomyocyte
  parenchyma; if these do **not** recover, that is a red flag for the deconvolution→DE pipeline (the bulk
  signal is large and unambiguous), not a biological null.
- **Expected sex structure:** male-biased magnitude in most tissues; LUNG female-biased; frankly opposite
  sexes in WAT/lung/liver; cytokine timing female-early / male-late (MoTrPAC #21).
- **Yu (Tier C):** validates *which* blood cell types/programs are exercise-responsive, NOT direction —
  acute human single-bout ≠ chronic rat training; a direction reversal there neither confirms nor refutes.
- **Quiet tissues:** cortex/hippocampus are the weakest bulk responders; weak per-cell-type signal there is
  expected, strong signal warrants scrutiny.
- **Magnitude:** effects are expected SMALL per gene (Vetr: ~52 genes/tissue >2 SDpheno; MoTrPAC: 56% of bulk
  fold-changes within 0.67–1.5×). Recovery is judged on direction-concordant significance, not large logFC.
