MoTrPAC training-regulated ('primary') genes still NOT tokenizable after the
3-bridge gene-ID liftover  (direct + symbol + Entrez/RGD ID-history)
=============================================================================

495 of the 9,800 training-regulated transcriptome genes remain without a
GeneCompass token: 8,768 already in vocab + 498 symbol-recovered + 39
id_history-recovered = 9,305 covered (95.0%). The full per-gene record is
motrpac_missed_genes.tsv, flagged by category and importance (n_tissues x
curation): high 25, med 38, low 432.

DEFINITIVE answer to "can we recover these by GROWING the GeneCompass vocab?":
  NO -- not from our current single-cell corpus. Using the RGD map (reliable,
  unlike the biomart Gene-name column which is empty for 43% of rat genes),
  472/495 of the still-missed genes resolve to a current mRatBN7.2 ENSRNOG,
  but 0/495 occur in ANY of our single-cell references (in_sc_corpus column).
  So these genes are simply absent from our SC data; minting GeneCompass tokens
  for them would require deeper/different single-cell data (the reference
  problem), not vocabulary growth. Notable members: the RT1-* rat-MHC immune
  cluster (training-regulated in up to 8 tissues) and a handful of real
  metabolic/immune genes (CD36, NDUFA13, TPI1, GPT, LYVE1, C4A, CFB).

Columns: symbol, old_ensrnog, current_ensrnog (via RGD), n_tissues_trainreg,
tissues, in_current_annot, in_sc_corpus, category, importance.
